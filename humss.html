Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS)

   This strand encompasses a wide range of disciplines that investigate and inquire into human situations through empirical, analytical, and critical ways of examining human behavior and societal developments. Students obtaining college degrees in this field will have a better grasp of the arts, culture, literature, politics, and society, as well as how the complex interplay of various components helps them appreciate individual behavior and social groups, as well as the current concerns that accompany them.

   Social Science, on the other hand, is an academic field that studies how people interact with society, institutions, and the larger world. The social sciences include various sub-disciplines such as psychology, economics, political science, and sociology.

   People who take HUMSS classes learn skills that are highly desired in the business, such as persuasive communication, research skills, analytical ability, and critical thinking that affects decision-making.

What are the themes covered in HUMSS, and what can you expect to learn? The HUMSS strand curriculum is comprehensively equipped to provide students with the necessary skills for further studies in higher educational institutions, as well as their day-to-day operations in careers that rely on rapport and immersion with fellowmen. Our senior high school students can anticipate to study the following major subjects in HUMSS:

Communication
Literature
Politics
Natural Sciences
The Social Sciences

Our students are well-prepared for possible employment in a variety of industries, including medicine and law, thanks to these abilities and their developed knowledge bases.
