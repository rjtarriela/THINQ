General Academic Strand (GAS)

   The General Academic Strand is designed for students who are unsure of what course or degree they wish to pursue in college. This strand was created so that indecisive students might continue with any college program. However, learners in this strand may or may not take bridge programs for subjects not studied during Senior High (depending on the school).

Unlike other strands, the senior high school GAS provides pupils with a broad overview of various courses rather than a concentrated approach. Students in this strand receive a comprehensive education that covers subjects from all three strands - HUMSS, STEM, and ABM. General academic strand subjects are appropriate for students who seek a well-rounded educational experience that prepares them for a variety of job and education paths following graduation. The Benefits of GAS

The GAS exposes senior high school students to a number of disciplines, allowing them to examine each one to determine which one is the best fit for them. Students can use GAS to:

Consider a variety of topics such as disaster preparedness, risk reduction, and organizational management.
Enjoy a diverse range of studies from the other SHS strands, including HUMSS, ABM, and STEM. Because they have learned about a variety of courses, students may make a more educated decision for their undergraduate program and potential professional path.
