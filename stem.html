Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM)

   Science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) education is a national priority because it fosters innovation, supports economic development, and improves our understanding of the world. A high-quality STEM education equips students with the knowledge and skills they need to solve problems, collect and analyze evidence, and make sense of data. STEM activities at IES increase the study and implementation of effective instructional approaches while also tracking student learning improvement.

   This strand will develop students' abilities to evaluate simple to complex societal problems and to be responsive and active in the formulation of their solutions through the application and integration of scientific, technological, engineering, and mathematical concepts as it prepares them to pursue college degrees that focus on the enigma of the natural world, thereby leading them to become future scientists, technological analysts and experts, engineers, mathematicians, and other professionals.

The STEM strand gives senior high school students exposure to the intertwining disciplines (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) to see how they work in real life. The STEM strand:

  • Builds resilience in a safe environment by allowing students to experiment and to naturally experience failures as part of the learning process
  • Encourages teamwork so students can find solutions to problems, record data, write reports, give presentations, and prepare them for STEM strand jobs
  • Teaches kids about the power and importance of technology, especially in today’s digital age
  • Fosters a habit of adaption when things don’t go as planned
